Why lernOS?

  • Driver 1: More and more change in all aspects of life
  • Driver 2: Changes in the working environment through automation
  • Driver 3: Demographic change and labour shortage of skilled workers
  • Approach: Promotion of informal learning at all levels of society

Informal Learning

  1. Is not institutionally organized
  2. Has little structured
  3. Occurs everyday
  4. Is self-directed by learners
  5. Is not accompanied pedagogically
  6. Includes learning from experience & reflection
  7. Is a conscious process
  8. Has capacity to act as its goal
  9. Is often embedded in a social context

Google’s Innovative Mindset

Google proved that you could systematize innovation and create an environment where you could ask why things are the way they are, and wonder if they can be done in a different way - where you look outside your own field for an idea.

Early on we put in a system of mechanisms for innovating, the so-called 70/20/10 system. This was a principle that everyone should spend 70% of their time on their core job, 20% as part of another team, and 10% on something blue sky.

The critical issue is to build the habits of mind, from the executive suite to the shop floor, that encourage an innovative mindset,
living in a world of continuous questioning and re-learning.

Eric Schmidt - Ex-CEO of Google
